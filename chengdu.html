Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan province, is a laid-back city famous for its fiery cuisine and as the home of giant pandas. A UNESCO City of Gastronomy, Chengdu offers a paradise for food lovers with its signature spicy hot pot, Mapo Tofu, and countless street snacks. Beyond the food, you can visit tranquil teahouses and the famous Panda Research Base.

Spicy Boiled Beef

Thin beef poached in a chile-and-peppercorn broth, topped with hot oil and aromatics.

In Sichuan, cooks developed a “water-boiled” method that keeps beef tender while packing it with chile heat and peppercorn aroma. The broth is poured over the meat, then hot oil poured over garlic and scallions right before serving.

Mapo Tofu

Silky tofu in a spicy, numbing sauce with fermented beans, chiles, and fragrant oil.

The dish began at a small Chengdu eatery in the late Qing dynasty. The balance of tofu softness, fermented bean depth, chile heat, and peppercorn tingle made it a classic. Today it’s one of Chengdu’s most famous comfort foods.

Hot Pot

Boiling broth at the table, bright with chiles and peppercorns, for cooking meats and vegetables.

Shared pots go back centuries in China, but Chengdu’s modern, peppercorn-forward style made the chile-and-peppercorn broth famous. Diners cook meats and vegetables at the table and mix their own garlic-sesame-chile sauces—a spicy, social ritual that defines local nights out.
